Device Structure
* Architecture: 2D planar N-channel MOSFET
* Dimensions: 1.0 µm channel length, 1.0 µm channel width, and a 20 nm gate oxide thickness
* Doping: Heavily doped n-type source/drain (10²⁰ cm⁻³) set within a moderately doped p-type silicon substrate (10¹⁶ cm⁻³)

Key Results
* Extracted Parameters: Successfully extracted a zero-bias threshold voltage of 0.676 V and a subthreshold swing of 79.37 mV/decade
* Switching Performance: Achieved an excellent ON/OFF current ratio of 4.16 x 10¹⁰
* Internal Physics: Verified drift-diffusion transport mechanisms and the elimination of the energy barrier in the ON state through internal electrostatic potential and energy band analysis
* Secondary Effects: Accurately characterized critical behaviors including channel length modulation, the body effect, and mobility degradation

Software Requirements
* Silvaco Atlas TCAD
* Silvaco TonyPlot
* nanoHUB Platform

Key Findings
The simulation successfully modeled the fundamental solid-state physics of an N-channel MOSFET, enabling the direct and derived extraction of SPICE Level-1 model parameters. The analysis highlighted real-world device behaviors that diverge from ideal analytical models, demonstrating that effective surface mobility is significantly reduced due to interface scattering, and that 2D lateral fringing fields effectively increase the body effect coefficient beyond 1D estimations.
